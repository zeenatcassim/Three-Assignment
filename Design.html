Design Section

Building a website takes a lot of time. You first need to design wireframes for how it is going to look, then you need to add visual elements such as colour, font, etc to make the website look good, decide on what UI and UX interactions should be present in the website and then finally you are almost done.

Style Guide

The colours I used for assignment 1 were blue, purple, white, grey and black. They were cool and calming colours that I wanted to use to produce a calming effect on the user. However, for this, assignment 3, I used warm colours such Vivid Tangerine (#F3958B), Linen (#FAEEDF), Bone (#EBD7C5), Pastel Blue (#A7D1D0) and Nickel (#737373). I found this colour scheme on the website, www.schemecolor.com.

I changed the colour scheme because I found that brighter warm colours gives off a playful mood and feeling to the website. It makes the website look creative, stylish and welcoming. The previous colours made the website look dull and serious. It ended up looking non-friendly and unapproachable. With this new colours, the website looks user friendly and evokes a pleasant mood for the user.

Last assignment I used one font throughout the website to make the content look easy and accessible to read. However, this time, I experimented with many fonts and ended up using more than one type throughout the website. I used one font for the big catchy headings and another for the webpage content. This ended up contributing to the friendly and playful mood that the website gives off. For simplicity’s sake, I stuck with a maximum of two fonts to avoid overwhelming the user.

This also creates a convention and adds to the hierarchy of information, users become used to seeing a big heading with a small sentence below it at first glance at the webpage. When they scroll down, then they see the webpages content.

I didn’t drastically change the layout of the website from assignment 1. It is mostly the same but in assignment 3, I changed the font sizes, font types, backgrounds and content placing. This assignment, instead of focusing on a simple and straightforward design. I aimed for a creative, friendly and playful design. All webpages had a big heading, small sentence below it and a background below both these elements. After this point, the content placing changed depending on what the webpage was for but I used the convention, from assignment 1, of having content shown on top of a background colour.

UI and UX

Other UX and UI decisions include colour, font and negative space decisions but this has all been explained above.

The navigation bar is centred towards the middle. The buttons are big and have no borders to make the edges look smooth and for it to blend with the button background colour. This makes it look smooth and is easy for the reader to look at.

When the reader hovers above the navigation buttons, it enlarges and the colour changes. This increases the reader’s focus on the button and shows that it is interactable. This tells the reader that they can click on the button, in other words, that this is a point of interaction. However, this has not only been done for the navigation buttons, this functionality is also present for the buttons, to read a specific weekly blog, at the weekly blog webpage and when the reader want to click the scroll up button.

Other points of interaction include certain text in a paragraph. Words that lead the reader to a certain part of the webpage are highlighted in a different colour and when the reader hovers above that word, the font increases. This is to make user flow easy. Instead of reading one big paragraph and then looking at the different choices of buttons to click on, it is easier for the reader to decided whether to click on a highlighted text from the paragraph they are already reading.
